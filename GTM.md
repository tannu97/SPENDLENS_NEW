# Go-To-Market Strategy — SpendLens

## Exact Target User

**Primary**: Engineering Manager or CTO at a 5–30 person B2B SaaS startup. Series A or seed-stage. They approved AI tool subscriptions in 2023-2024 when "AI productivity" was the hot narrative, and haven't re-evaluated since. They're not overspending carelessly — they just never had a reason to audit.

**Secondary**: Founder-engineer at a solo or 2-3 person dev shop. They're paying for Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus, and Cursor Pro individually. They don't realize they have an overlap problem.

**What they Google/scroll before wanting this tool**:
- "is cursor worth it vs github copilot"
- "best AI coding tools for small teams 2025"
- "chatgpt team plan vs plus difference"
- Reddit threads: r/cursor, r/ChatGPT, r/ExperiencedDevs on AI tool comparisons

## Where They Hang Out Online

- **r/ExperiencedDevs**, **r/cscareerquestions** — debates on AI tool ROI
- **Slack**: Rands Leadership Slack (engineering leaders), Indie Hackers Slack
- **Discord**: Cursor Discord (25k+ members), Windsurf Discord
- **X/Twitter**: follow @levelsio, @paulg, @benedictevans — startup/productivity discourse
- **Hacker News**: Show HN, Ask HN threads on AI tooling cost
- **Linear/Notion communities**: ops-minded builders who track software costs

## Getting the First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1: Seeding**
- Post "Show HN: I built a free AI tool spend auditor for startups" — target top 20 HN front page. Include a 60-second Loom demo. HN loves tools that solve real financial pain with transparency.
- DM 20 technical founders in the Rands Leadership Slack with: "Hey, I built a thing that found $320/mo in savings for a team like yours in 90 seconds. Want to try it?"

**Week 2: Community Drop**
- Post in Cursor Discord's #general: "Made a free tool that tells you if you're overpaying for Cursor + Copilot together — most teams paying for both are wasting $100+/mo. Took 90 seconds to check mine." Real, personal, not spammy.
- Post same in Windsurf Discord.
- Write a 400-word Twitter thread: "I audited 10 startup AI stacks this week. Here's what I found:" — with the 3 most surprising findings (overlap being the biggest). Link SpendLens at the end.

**Week 3: Amplification**
- Identify 5 startup finance/ops blogs (Firstround Review, Lenny's Newsletter comments, Calm Fund) and offer a guest post or quote about the findings.
- Post the aggregate anonymized data: "30 startups audited: average overspend $280/mo. Here's the breakdown by tool."

**Week 4: Retention Loop**
- Email everyone who submitted a lead: "Q2 pricing update — 2 tools you're on just changed pricing. Re-run your audit." Creates a re-engagement reason.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already sells AI credits to companies. Their existing customers are the exact overspenders SpendLens identifies. Credex can email their customer list: "Free audit tool — find out how much you're leaving on the table (and how Credex credits close the gap)." That's a warm channel with a trusted sender — no cold acquisition cost.
